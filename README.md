# 📂 Orchestra ROI Engine: Technical Reference & Handbook

This document outlines the financial engineering, logic, and standardized assumptions used to calculate the **Return on Investment (ROI)** for Orchestra’s AI-driven industrial products.

<hr>

## 1. The Core Calculation Framework

The engine operates on two parallel financial tracks: **Throughput Expansion** (Increasing top-line revenue) and **Cost Recovery** (Decreasing operational waste).

### A. The Baseline: OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)

OEE is the gold standard for measuring manufacturing productivity. It is the product of three independent operational levers:

$$OEE = \text{Availability (A)} \times \text{Performance (P)} \times \text{Quality (Q)}$$

### B. Ideal Revenue Potential ($R_{ideal}$)

This represents the theoretical maximum revenue a factory could generate if it operated at 100% efficiency.

$$R_{ideal} = \frac{\text{Current Annual Revenue}}{\text{Current OEE}}$$

<hr>

## 2. Revenue Gain Logic (Throughput Profit)

This calculates the specific profit generated by "unlocking" time currently lost to bottlenecks, human hesitation, or machine micro-stops.

$$\text{Gain} = (R_{ideal} \times \Delta OEE) \times \text{Gross Margin}$$

> **Note:** $\Delta OEE$ represents the absolute percentage lift provided by the Orchestra AI deployment (e.g., moving from 50% to 60% OEE is a $\Delta OEE$ of 0.10).

<hr>

## 3. Product-Specific ROI Logic

Each Orchestra product is engineered to "attack" a different lever of the P&L statement.

### P1: Product A — Expert Skill (Precision Focus)

* **Primary Lever:** Quality (Q) & Material Cost.
* **Logic:** Reduces the volume of raw material discarded due to human-driven cutting or assembly errors.
* **Equation:**
$$\text{Total Return} = (\text{Material Cost} \times \text{Scrap Reduction \%}) + \text{Throughput Gain}$$

* **Assumption:** Material Cost is derived as: $\text{Revenue} \times (1 - \text{Gross Margin})$.

<hr>

### P2: Product B — Workflow (Speed Focus)

* **Primary Lever:** Performance (P).
* **Logic:** Increases the number of units produced per hour by eliminating "Labor Leakage" (non-value-added movement).
* **Equation:**
$$\text{Total Return} = (R_{ideal} \times \Delta P) \times \text{Gross Margin}$$

* **Context:** Product B is a "Volume Play," focusing strictly on maximizing top-line growth through speed.

<hr>

### P3: Product C — Risk & Compliance (Admin Focus)

* **Primary Lever:** Availability (A) & Indirect Labor.
* **Logic:** Reclaims manual labor hours spent on safety reporting and prevents line-stops caused by compliance breaches.
* **Equation:**
$$\text{Total Return} = (\text{Admin Hours Saved} \times 12 \times \text{Labor Rate}) + \text{Throughput Profit}$$

# 🧮 Manual ROI Validation Map: Product A (Textiles)

This section provides a transparent "Hand-Check" of the mathematical logic used in the Orchestra Engine, adjusted for industrial reality with a **70% Realization Factor**.

---

### 1. Baseline Assumptions (The "Current" State)
* **Total Annual Revenue ($R_{total}$):** $2 \text{ Lines} \times \$800,000 = \mathbf{\$1,600,000}$
* **Gross Margin:** $12\%$
* **Current OEE:** $0.70 (A) \times 0.50 (P) \times 0.80 (Q) = \mathbf{28.0\%}$
* **Material Cost Base:** $\$1,600,000 \times (1 - 0.12) = \mathbf{\$1,408,000}$
* **Ideal Potential ($R_{ideal}$):** $\$1,600,000 / 0.28 = \mathbf{\$5,714,286}$

---

### 2. The Orchestra Impact (Theoretical AI Lifts)
We apply two distinct value levers: **Direct Material Savings** and **Efficiency Throughput**.

**A. Direct Material Savings (Quality Focus)**
* *Logic:* $\text{Material Cost} \times \text{Scrap Reduction \%}$
* *Calculation:* $\$1,408,000 \times 0.07 = \mathbf{\$98,560}$

**B. Throughput Profit Gain (OEE Lift)**
* *New Target OEE:* $0.70 \times 0.50 \times (0.80 + 0.04) = \mathbf{29.4\%}$
* *Absolute OEE Lift ($\Delta OEE$):* $29.4\% - 28.0\% = \mathbf{1.4\%}$
* *Logic:* $(R_{ideal} \times \Delta OEE) \times \text{Gross Margin}$
* *Calculation:* $(\$5,714,286 \times 0.014) \times 0.12 = \mathbf{\$9,600}$

---

### 3. The Reality Adjustment (70% Realization Buffer)
To account for human response time and operational slack, we assume only **70%** of the theoretical gain is captured.

* **Gross Theoretical Impact:** $\$98,560 + \$9,600 = \$108,160$
* **Realized Annual Impact:** $\$108,160 \times 0.70 = \mathbf{\$75,712}$

---

### 4. Final 5-Year Net Wealth Summary 
We calculate the total realized gain over 5 years and subtract the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**.

* **5-Year Gross Realized Gain:** $\$75,712 \times 5 = \mathbf{\$378,560}$
* **Total Maintenance/Sub (OpEx):** $\$7,000 \times 5 = \mathbf{\$35,000}$
* **Initial Setup (CapEx):** $\mathbf{\$35,000}$

#### **The 5-Year Net Result:**
$$\text{Net Return} = (\text{5-Year Gain}) - (\text{Total OpEx}) - (\text{Initial CapEx})$$
$$\$378,560 - \$35,000 - \$35,000 = \mathbf{\$308,560}$$

**Strategic Note:** The investment of $70,000 (Total TCO) generates over $300,000 in realized profit, representing a **4.4x return on total spend** over 5 years.
